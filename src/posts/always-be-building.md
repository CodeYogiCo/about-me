This is the first post on this site. It feels right that it's about building — because that's the loop everything else fits into.

I keep a notebook. Not a fancy one; a plain text file in a folder called `notes/`. In it, I write down systems I want to build but probably never will. There are around eighty of them now. A handful turned into things. Most didn't.

For a long time I thought the unfinished ones were a kind of failure. They aren't. They're the cheapest education I've found.

## what gets written down

A typical entry is two paragraphs. The first describes the system I'd build if I had a free weekend: a tiny database with an unusual storage layout, a CLI that does one strange thing, a service that exists for ten users. The second is the part that matters — the **why**. What problem would this fix? What would the world look like if it existed?

Most of the time, the second paragraph is the actual work. By the time I've written it, I either know the system isn't worth building, or I know what the next experiment is. Either outcome is a win.

## what stays in the notebook

A short list of what I've learned from systems I never shipped:

- The hard part of search is rarely the index. It's keeping the index honest.
- Storage formats are values. Once you write data on disk, you've committed to a vocabulary.
- Most systems are 80% logging. The 20% that does the actual job is the easy part.
- Boring is debuggable. Clever is a debt.

None of these would fit on a slide. All of them are things I needed to almost-build something to internalize.

## why "always"

The "always" in the title isn't about hustle. It's about staying in the loop where you're a beginner at something most weeks. The strongest engineers I know never feel like experts; they feel like they're catching up.

> If I'm not at least mildly confused by a thing I'm working on, I've drifted toward the comfortable middle. The middle is fine. It's just not where the interesting work lives.

So this site is partly the notebook spilling into public. Some of what gets posted here will be polished. Some of it won't be. Some of it will be wrong, and I'll edit it later.

That's fine. The point is to stay building.
